About myself

I have had a passion for lighting from a very young age. Every time I went to see a show I would spend half of the time looking at the lighting above, and wonder the possibilities such a rjg may have, and how all of the technology worked. My lighting journey then continued in late 2019, when I started planning the core concepts of lighting for shows in my school. This then led to later operating lighting for their Christmas Concert in 2023. Currently, I work with Young Technicians Academy and Limelight Academy of Performing Art (LAPA) to design and operate lighting for their amateur theatre shows. I have worked with them since late 2024, and look forward to working with them over then upcoming months. My aim for the future is to be a freelance lighting designer, designing and operating lighting for all types of shows from concert touring to west end theatre shows.

What do I do?

I design, plan and operate lighting for shows. I incorporate my client’s visions, while implementing the necessary technical information required for your show. I use popular software such as ChamSys MagicQ and Avolites Titan which are both well known and well loved throughout the industry to control lighting rigs. I also use the popular lighting visualizer ‘Capture’ to design lighting for shows, which can be used to render eye catching designs and produce detailed lighting plots. I have experience with protocols such as DMX and ArtNet, which are industry standard for lighting networking.

Tools I use

I use popular lighting software such as ChamSys MagicQ and Avolites Titan to program and operate lighting for shows. I use Capture for visualising and producing lighting plots for shows.

Why do I do it?

I believe that no matter what your show is, the right lighting is key. I am able to apply my technical knowledge to my client's visions and create the right lighting design for them.
